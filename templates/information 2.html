Understanding Medications

Medications play a vital role in treating, managing, and preventing diseases. They work in different ways, depending on their class and purpose, such as killing bacteria, relieving pain, or reducing fever.

The diagram on the left illustrates how medications interact with the body. For example, antibiotics target bacterial cells without harming human cells, while antihistamines block histamine receptors to reduce allergy symptoms.

Always consult a healthcare provider to ensure proper usage of medications, including the correct dosage, potential side effects, and interactions with other drugs.
